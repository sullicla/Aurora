What is Data Center?

Data centers are places where digital information is stored, transferred, and accessed. They have physical or virtual servers that are connected using networking equipment.

Sending data is a lot like sending a letter. When you use your device, like your phone or computer, to access the internet, the data you send is like the letter. It gets put into a digital envelope with an address on it called an IP address. The data then travels through a series of connections, like phone lines or fiber optic cables, until it reaches the data center.

Once the data arrives at the data center, it gets stored on big computers called servers. These servers are like big filing cabinets that can hold lots of information. Then when you want to access the data again, it travels back to your device through the same series of connections.
